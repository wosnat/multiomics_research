### Exploration — Final ranking [KG]

| Rank | Ontology | Genome cov | Best level | Level cov | Terms | Median | Max |
|:---:|----------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **1** | **cyanorak_role** | **78%** | **1** | **80%** | **110** | **9** | **340** |

| 4 | ec | 44% | 1 | 43% | 61 | 7 | 91 |
| 5 | kegg | 57% | 1 | 41% | 38 | 14 | 164 |
| 6-9 | cog, go_cc, pfam, tigr | — | none | — | — | — | — |

[interpretation] CyanoRak level 1 is the clear winner:
- 80% genome coverage — highest among ontologies with a qualifying level
- 110 terms with median 9 genes — good statistical power for Fisher's exact
- Curated for cyanobacteria — biologically meaningful categories (nitrogen metabolism, photosynthesis, transport, ribosomal proteins)
- Max 340 is one catch-all (`R.2` Conserved hypothetical proteins) — won't produce false enrichment

[interpretation] Initial ranking (before genome_coverage metric) incorrectly selected CyanoRak level 2. A level can have ideal term sizes but only cover 18% of the genome. The per-level genome_coverage metric is essential for hierarchy level selection. Bug fixed in `survey_ontology`.

[interpretation] KEGG is interesting: 41% genome coverage is constant across all levels — genes that connect to the hierarchy are the same at every level. The gap is likely genes with KO annotations but no pathway linkage. Worth investigating in future B analysis.

[interpretation] GO is not competitive — even at levels with decent coverage, terms range from massive (1,038g) to tiny (1g) with no usable middle ground. The DAG structure means ancestor terms absorb most genes.

### Decision
**Select CyanoRak level 1 for B1 enrichment.** KEGG subcategory in backlog for B2. The genome_coverage metric was essential for correct level selection — added to `survey_ontology` as a permanent feature.
