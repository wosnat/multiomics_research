**GO BP:** Massive redundancy. Level 0 = single root (1,119 genes). Even at level 3-4, median is 4-5 genes/term ‚Äî too sparse for power. Massive max values (635, 187) from broad ancestor terms. Not competitive.

**EC:** 42% coverage. Level 1 has median 7 (decent) but only covers enzymes ‚Äî misses structural, regulatory, transport proteins.

[interpretation] CyanoRak level 2 is the clear winner for B1:
- 26 terms with median 11.5 genes/term ‚Äî right in the sweet spot for Fisher's exact
- 78% genome coverage ‚Äî the 22% unannotated are mostly hypothetical proteins
- Curated for cyanobacteria ‚Äî categories are biologically meaningful for Prochlorococcus (nitrogen metabolism, photosynthesis, transport)
- Clean 3-level hierarchy, no DAG complexity

[interpretation] KEGG subcategory is a solid second choice for B2 ‚Äî broader scope (metabolic pathways beyond cyanobacteria-specific), but 57% coverage means more invisible genes.

[interpretation] GO is not competitive for MED4 enrichment ‚Äî even at the best levels, most terms have 1-5 genes. Too sparse.

### Decision
**Select CyanoRak level 2 for B1 enrichment.** KEGG subcategory in backlog for B2. CyanoRak level 1 (109 terms, median 9) as alternate granularity for sensitivity analysis.
