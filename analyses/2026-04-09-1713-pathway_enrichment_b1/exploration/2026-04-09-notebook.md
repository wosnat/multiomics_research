[interpretation] CyanoRak level 1 is the clear winner:
- 99% gene coverage at the level — nearly all annotated genes are reachable
- 109 terms with median 9 genes — good power for Fisher's exact
- 78% genome coverage — highest among ontologies with a qualifying level
- Curated for cyanobacteria — categories are biologically meaningful (nitrogen metabolism, photosynthesis, transport)

[interpretation] Initial ranking (before gene coverage metric) incorrectly selected CyanoRak level 2. The gene_coverage metric is essential for hierarchy level selection — a level can have ideal term sizes but only cover a fraction of the biology. Bug fixed in `survey_ontology`.

[interpretation] KEGG is interesting: 72% gene coverage is constant across all levels — the genes that connect to the hierarchy are the same at every level. The 28% that don't connect may be KEGG-annotated at the KO level but not linked to any pathway. Worth investigating in a future B analysis.

### Decision
**Select CyanoRak level 1 for B1 enrichment.** KEGG subcategory in backlog for B2. The gene_coverage metric was essential for correct level selection.
